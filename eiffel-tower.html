Here are some facts about Paris:

Paris, France's capital, earned the "City of Light" nickname for being an early adopter of street lighting and a hub during the Enlightenment. The city is home to the Louvre, the world's largest art museum, and was originally a Roman settlement called Lutetia. The iconic Eiffel Tower was a temporary structure for the 1889 World's Fair, and Paris has no stop signs. Below the city's surface lie the Catacombs, containing the remains of six million people.
